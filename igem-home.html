Feline diabetes is a prevalent issue known to affect around 1 in 230 house cats. Late treatment often results in a higher overall cost to the owners along with severe discomfort for the cat. The current detection method consists of urine and/or glucose test(s) done by the felineâ€™s vet if symptoms are observed, and the disease is often caught too late in these cases.

Huge emotional and financial strain on owners

  • Top 3 cause of death in cats
  • Over one million cats diagnosed
  • Owners pay hundreds of dollars per year

Our project provides an early diabetes detection system by providing a visual glucose level indicator in the cats litter. Earlier detection will reduce the costs of long-term treatment and increase the effectiveness of current treatments by providing pet owners with a way to consistently monitor the condition.
